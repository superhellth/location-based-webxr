GPS+SLAM — Location-Based WebXR

Build location-based Augmented Reality experiences that run in the mobile browser — no app install. An Apache-2.0 framework fuses GPS with WebXR odometry so content can sit on a specific spot on a path, a wall, or the ground, with accuracy beyond raw GPS.

Anchor Starter Demo Place a GPS anchor, reload, and watch it re-localise at the exact physical spot. Minimal Example The smallest three.js + GPS+AR starting point: tap to place a GPS anchor next to a naive floater and watch the difference. Recorder The full app to evaluate tracking accuracy: capture AR + GPS sessions on a phone, replay and inspect alignment on a desktop.

Why GPS+SLAM?

  • Accuracy beyond raw GPS: fuses GPS with the device's AR odometry, so alignment gets more stable as the user walks — visible drift drops well below raw GPS.
  • Just a URL, no app install: the whole experience runs in the mobile browser through WebXR.
  • Fully offline alignment: localises from the device's own GPS, camera tracking, and motion sensors — no VPS provider or network scan of the place required.
  • Anchors for shareable content: tie objects to a GPS coordinate for replay, persistence, and sharing.

What you can build

  • Outdoor AR navigation with GPS-anchored arrows and waypoints.
  • Persistent 3D content dropped at lat/lon that stays put.
  • AR tour guides, museum trails, and location-based games.
  • Field-data capture tools for GPS, AR poses, frames, and depth.
